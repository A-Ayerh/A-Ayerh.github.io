  • Beta Waves (13-30 Hz): These waves are linked to active thinking, problem-solving, and motor control. They will be particularly important for mapping brain activity during motor tasks.

Exploring Spatial Mapping Algorithms

The choice of spatial mapping algorithm is critical to the success of the project. Initially, we will explore simpler algorithms to get started with the EEG data, with a plan to transition to more advanced models as the project develops.

  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA): PCA is a simple dimensionality reduction algorithm that helps us reduce the noise in the EEG data and focus on the most important spatial patterns in the brain's electrical activity.
  • t-SNE (t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding): t-SNE is a powerful tool for visualizing high-dimensional EEG data. It will be useful in representing brain activity in a lower-dimensional space for easier visualization of spatial patterns.
  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs): CNNs are often used for image recognition, but they are also excellent for analyzing spatial data from EEG signals, treating the electrode positions as 2D data points for recognizing patterns.
